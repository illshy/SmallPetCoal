The Issue

Every Paw Deserves Equal Treatment

Small dogs bring immense joy to millions of families worldwide. Yet, despite their compact size and manageable nature, they face discrimination that larger breeds often don't encounter.

From "no pets" policies that ignore the practicality of small dogs to restaurants that won't allow a well-behaved Chihuahua in a carrier bag, the restrictions are both widespread and unjust.

Understanding The Problem

Where Small Dogs Face Discrimination

Despite their size and ease of management, small dogs encounter barriers in three major areas of daily life.

Housing Discrimination

Many landlords implement blanket "no pet" policies, refusing to consider that a 5-pound Yorkie is vastly different from a 100-pound Great Dane.

  • "No pets allowed" regardless of size
  • Excessive pet deposits for tiny dogs
  • Weight limits that unfairly exclude

The Facts

Why These Restrictions Are Unjustified

Small dogs are uniquely suited for urban living and public spaces. The restrictions they face are often based on outdated policies and misconceptions.

Small dogs typically weigh under 20 pounds and can be comfortably carried in bags or on laps. They require minimal space and are easy to control in any environment.

When properly contained, small dogs are virtually unnoticeable in public settings. They don't bump into people, don't require large spaces, and many are trained to be quiet and calm.

Small breeds like Poodles, Papillons, and Miniature Schnauzers rank among the most intelligent dogs. They can be trained to behave impeccably in any situation.

Small dogs pose virtually no physical threat. Even in worst-case scenarios, their size means they cannot cause significant harm, yet they're often subject to the same restrictions as larger breeds.

Make A Difference

How to Fight Discrimination

Together, we can change policies and attitudes. Here's how you can help.

01

Advocate

Speak up when you encounter unfair policies. Politely challenge restrictions and explain why small dogs deserve consideration.

  • Talk to business owners about their policies
  • Write to your local representatives
  • Share your experiences on social media
02

Educate

Help dispel myths about small dogs. Share facts about their behavior, training capabilities, and suitability for various environments.

  • Share educational content online
  • Bring well-behaved dogs to public spaces
  • Host community information sessions
03

Know Your Rights

Understand the legal protections available to pet owners. Many discriminatory policies can be challenged legally.

  • Research local pet ownership laws
  • Document discriminatory incidents
  • Consult with pet advocacy groups
04

Build Community

Connect with other small dog owners. Together, your voice is stronger and your impact greater.

  • Join local pet owner groups
  • Attend pet-friendly events
  • Support pet-friendly businesses
